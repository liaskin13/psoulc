# PRODUCT.md — Pleasant Soul Collective

## Register
product

## Users
- **D (Darnell / Soul Pleasant)** — The artist. Primary user. Master tier. God Mode. Sees his sovereign console with green identity (`#14dc14`). Uses the platform to upload, tag, organize, and broadcast his work.
- **L (Lisa)** — The Architect. Co-creator and platform builder. Master tier. God Mode Plus. Admin, IT, marketing, gatekeeper, security. Sees her architect console with cyan identity.
- **Muses** (Janet, Erikah, Larry, Drake) — Tier C. Lockbox artists. They have their own vaults with unique identity colors. Access via lockbox codes.
- **Members/Subscribers** — Paying tier. Access to vault content D has chosen to share.
- **Listeners** — Free/public viewers. Need a better name — something that hints they are not fully part of the collective but makes them *want* to be. Do not use "Listeners" in new design work.
- **Collaborators** — Vault-access, vetted contributors. Phase 11 onboarding flow.

## Product Purpose
A cinematic music streaming and sharing platform where the independent artist is the center of the universe. D's private archive — a room built for the music, and for the people D chose to let in. Not a public streaming service. Not a discovery platform. Sovereign infrastructure for a specific artist and his world.

## Memorable Thing
"This is D's world — I'm just visiting." AND: "Looks like nothing anyone has ever seen."

## Brand Voice
Achromatic Brutalist Futurism. The platform has no color identity of its own — it is pure black architecture. The artist brings the color. Sovereign. Precise. Cinematic. Never decorative. Never algorithmic.

## UX Reference
The General Master Console is the single base template for all Master-tier consoles. Structural reference: Serato DJ library model — vault wall, rail, track detail panel, waveform display — adapted for a single-deck archive management context, not a mixing surface. Both D and L consoles build from this one template. Identity colour is a 3-point overlay on top of the base. Serato is the structural reference only; this platform looks nothing like Serato's 20-year-old software aesthetic.

D's 3 identity points: THE SIGNAL button · active track accent line · BPM/Key in track detail panel. Green (`#14dc14`).
L's 3 identity points: same 3 locations. Cyan (`#00e5ff`).
Listener view: no identity colour. Guest in D's world. Achromatic only.

## Anti-References
- No Spotify, SoundCloud, Apple Music UI patterns
- No social metrics (likes, follower counts, play counts)
- No algorithmic recommendations
- No rounded corners, gradient blobs, glassmorphism as default
- No space/astronomy themes (banned permanently)
- No aurora light shows, animated hero gradients, gradient text
- No amber anywhere on any surface — amber is permanently retired
- No identity colour beyond the 3 designated spots — identity colour spread across a surface is wallpaper, not sovereignty
- No CSS-only cosmetics presented as a console implementation — `AnalogConsole.jsx` must be a real built component
- Never show D anything that has not been confirmed working in a real browser session

## Strategic Principles
1. Artist sovereignty first — every design decision must serve the creator
2. The platform's brand color is whoever you're visiting (identity layer)
3. Negative space is load-bearing — absence is deliberate
4. Instruments respond; they don't perform
5. One typeface (Chakra Petch), one voice, no competing personalities
6. One base, two overlays — D and L share the General Master Console template; identity colour is a 3-point overlay, nothing more
7. Rules already written must be read — DESIGN.md is law; every session reads it in full before touching any file
