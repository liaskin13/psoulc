# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**D** — Primary artist and architect. UBC music production degree. AKAI MPC III beta tester. DJ with 30+ mixes. Has all his audio files locally. Operates the console as a performance instrument, not a file manager. Expects hardware-grade precision and response.

**L** — Co-architect and gatekeeper. Infrastructure and access control. Maintains the platform, manages who enters.

**Muses** — Featured artists granted vault access by D. Invited collaborators with sovereignty over their own uploads.

**Shadow subscribers** — Listeners with paid access ($9/mo). They are guests in D's world. They consume, they do not configure.

## Product Purpose

A sovereign private music instrument. Not a streaming platform. Not a social network. Not SoundCloud with a darker theme.

D holds godlike control over his catalog — who accesses his music, when, how, and on what terms. The console is the cockpit of that control. Every listener enters on his terms. Every collaborator is hand-selected.

Success looks like: D uploads a mix, sets the terms, and it is experienced exactly as he intended — with no platform interference, no algorithmic suggestions, no branding that isn't his.

## Brand Personality

Sovereign · Architectural · Cinematic

The interface does not perform friendliness. It performs precision. The feel is less "music app" and more "hardware instrument with a screen." Every surface should feel like it was built for one specific person — because it was.

"This is D's world. I'm just visiting."

## Gold Standard

Serato DJ Pro + Pioneer rekordbox + AKAI MPC III.

These are the references to beat — not in feature parity, but in feel. The weight of a button press. The confidence of a waveform. The sense that the machine responds to the artist, not the other way around.

D is an AKAI MPC III beta tester. He knows what precision hardware feels like. The console must meet that bar.

## Anti-references

SoundCloud, Spotify, Tidal, Apple Music, Twitch — any platform that puts the algorithm between artist and audience.

Pioneer rekordbox UI patterns (cluttered, utilitarian gray). Serato's legacy layout where track lists are afterthoughts. Any dashboard that feels like SaaS. Any aesthetic that feels like someone ran a Figma template.

No space themes. No orbital imagery. No astronomical metaphors. These were tried and removed — they belong to a different project.

Nothing that looks like AI made it.

## Design Principles

1. **The console is an instrument.** Every control should feel weighted and intentional — like a hardware button, not a web form element. Precision over approachability.

2. **Sovereignty is structural, not decorative.** D's control over his catalog should be felt in the hierarchy of the interface — not explained in a tooltip.

3. **Identity is color.** The identity token system (--identity, --identity-dim, --identity-glow) IS the user. When D is in the room, it's green. When L is in the room, it's cyan. This is not a theme toggle. It is the presence of a person.

4. **The artist's work is the biggest thing on screen.** Track lists, waveforms, and cue points should dominate the layout. Controls are servants to the catalog, not competitors for space.

5. **Looks like nothing anyone has ever seen.** Every screen is a first-principles decision. Category reflexes — "DJ software is gray and blue" — are rejected before they land.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

High contrast is a natural outcome of the achromatic brutalist aesthetic. WCAG AA minimum for all text surfaces. Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion` — animations are additive, not structural. No color-only information encoding: cue markers use both color and position to distinguish banks.
