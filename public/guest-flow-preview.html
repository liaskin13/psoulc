DESIGN DECISIONS

REMOVED
Serato cue points — gone from guest view
D's cue structure is private. Guests never see it. The waveform shape is visible but its internal landmarks are not. What replaces it: voice comment markers — anchored by guests and L, not by D's production structure. Different information, different ownership.
VOICE COMMENTS — the feature
Timed voice notes at precise waveform positions
SoundCloud had timed text comments. Nobody has done timed voice with whisper-mode playback. Guests (and L) leave a voice note at a precise moment in the mix — not at the song level, not in a chat, but pinned to exactly 0:44:58 of that specific mix. The marker is a small warm diamond on the waveform. Tap it to read the auto-transcription. Tap HEAR IT to listen. /* Voice comment color — warm near-white Distinct from identity green on purpose: Comments are human. Identity is D's sovereignty. */ --vc: rgba(240,230,200,0.72);
VOICE COMMENTS — whisper mode
Mix continues · Voice plays at ~25% volume
HEAR IT plays the voice comment alongside the mix, not instead of it. The mix auto-ducks to ~25% while the voice plays, then fades back up over 2s. The sensation is exactly like someone leaning over and whispering to you while the music plays. The mix is never interrupted — the comment is a layer, not a takeover. // On HEAR IT: mixGainNode.gain.linearRampToValueAtTime(0.25, ctx.currentTime + 0.3) vcAudio.play() vcAudio.onended = () => { mixGainNode.gain.linearRampToValueAtTime(1.0, ctx.currentTime + 2.0) }
VOICE COMMENTS — access rules
Who can leave them, who can hear them
Phase 1: Only L (and D via console) can leave voice notes visible to guests. L curates what guests encounter.

Phase 2: Guests with a code can leave voice notes — but they appear in a pending state until L approves them for the guest view. The console shows the full unmoderated feed. Guests see the curated version.

Console only: D and L can hear all voice notes (including pending) directly in the console. No approval required for their own view.
VOICE COMMENTS — auto-transcription
Text is always the default. Voice is opt-in.
When a guest taps a comment marker, they see the transcript first. Always. HEAR IT is a secondary action — never automatic. Guests in a public space, on a call, or who simply don't want audio can still fully engage with every comment via text. The voice is the original artifact; the transcript is the accessible layer. // On voice note submission: POST /voice-comment { vaultId, trackId, timestamp, audioBlob } // Worker triggers transcription (Cloudflare AI or Whisper) // Stores: audio in R2, transcript in D1 // Guest response: { transcript, audioUrl, ts, author }
RISK — VAULT LANDING
5:42 at 82px — total runtime as hero
Five hours forty-two minutes of uninterrupted music. That's the statement. Not the vault name. The duration shows the scale of D's work before a single note plays. No other platform leads with runtime because they're scared the number will deter. PSC is the opposite: it's a commitment, and the right guests want to know what they're entering.
RISK — TRACK LIST
Waveform thumbnail = each mix's unique visual identity
Every mix row has a 52×26px waveform thumbnail. The shape of a 79-minute mix is visibly different from a 90-minute mix. No album art. No cover images. The waveform IS the visual identity of each session. Combined with the track number at 15px Space Mono, each row reads like a numbered limited edition, not a Spotify track row.
EUREKA — PLAYING STATE
Waveform + voice note markers = the full picture
The playing stage has two layers: the waveform (the music's shape) and the voice comment markers (the human response to specific moments). The markers are warm diamonds — small, quiet, warm-colored — distinct from the cold green identity system. Together: you can see the structure of D's mix AND where people have felt moved enough to speak. That's unprecedented.
